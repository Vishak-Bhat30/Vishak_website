Publications

ICML 2026 · Under Review

interwhen: A Generalizable Framework for Verifiable Reasoning with Test-time Monitors

Vishak K Bhat, Prateek Chanda, Ashmit Khandelwal, Maitreyi Swaroop, Subbarao Kambhampati, Vineeth N. Balasubramanian, Nagarajan Natarajan, Amit Sharma

A test-time verification framework for language models that enforces correctness with respect to a set of verifiers. The framework introduces verifier compute as a new axis for test-time scaling and provides a plug-and-play mechanism to improve instance-level reliability of reasoning systems without retraining.

Research Interests

LLM Reasoning & Verified Reasoning

Test-time verification, monitor-guided reasoning, and verifier-guided generation to ensure correctness of LLM reasoning traces at inference time.

LLM Compression & Optimization

Pruning, quantization, and knowledge distillation techniques to make large models smaller and faster without sacrificing quality.

Reinforcement Learning

Policy gradient methods, multi-armed bandits, and RL-based optimization for real-world decision-making problems.

Computer Vision

Semantic segmentation, object detection, self-supervised learning, and vision-language models for visual understanding.

Graph Neural Networks

Message-passing architectures and graph transformers for scientific computing, document understanding, and relational reasoning.

Causal Discovery

LLM-driven causal discovery frameworks, Partial Ancestral Graphs (PAG), and structure learning from observational data.

Multimodal Learning & VLMs

Vision-language models, multimodal reasoning, and diffusion models for cross-modal understanding and generation.

Efficient Fine-tuning of LLMs

Parameter-efficient methods like LoRA, adapters, and prompt-tuning for adapting large language models to downstream tasks.
